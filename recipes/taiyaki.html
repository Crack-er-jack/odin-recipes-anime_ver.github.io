Taiyaki

Description

A classic street vendor snack in Japan, Taiyaki is a warm fish-shaped cake with red bean filling. This waffle-like Japanese snack is very popular at street fairs and festivals in Japan.

Ingredients for Taiyaki Batter

  • 170 grams butter (6 ounces, 12 tablespoons)
  • 65 grams light brown sugar (2.3 ounces, 8 tablespoons)
  • 100 grams egg (2 eggs, 3.5 ounces)
  • 240 grams cake flour (8.5 ounces, 1 1/2 cup)
  • 6 grams baking powder (0.2 ounces, 2 teaspoons)
  • 155 grams warm milk (5.5 ounces, 2/3 cup)
  • 10 grams honey (0.35 ounces, 2 teaspoons)
  • 6 grams light soy sauce (0.2 ounces, 2 teaspoons)
  • 4 grams vanilla extract (0.14 ounces, 2 teaspoons)

Filling (Choose One)

  • Red Bean paste
  • Chocolate
  • Custard
  • Nutella
  • Cheese
  • Sweet Potato Paste

Steps

  1. Mix together the milk, honey, soy sauce, and vanilla extract and set aside.
  2. Cream the butter together with the sugar. Beat in one egg, then the other.
  3. Beat in half the flour, then the milk, then the other half of flour. Set your burner to between low and medium-low and begin to heat your taiyaki pan.
  4. Use a squeeze bottle to squeeze a pool of batter into the body, then the tail, and around the body again.
  5. Place the red bean insert or your filling of choice into the center of the taiyaki and press in halfway.
  6. Pour more batter over the red bean insert so that it's fully covered. Close the grill lid and begin to grill.
  7. The taiyaki are done when they are evenly golden brown. Remove with a pair of chopsticks.
  8. Serve while still warm and crunchy on the outside. Oishi!

Itadakimasu!
